Dr. Manu V. T.

Assistant Professor

Department of Cyber Security and Digital Forensics

National Forensic Sciences University, Bhopal

Teaching has always been close to my heart — a passion shaped by my mother, who served as a state award–winning teacher in the government school system. Inspired by her dedication and impact, I chose to pursue a life in academia where I could blend learning, discovery, and the joy of guiding future engineers.

My academic journey began with a B.Tech. in Computer Science and Engineering from NIT Calicut, followed by an M.Tech. from the University of Kerala, Department of Computer Science. I then completed my Ph.D. in Computer Science at the University of Hyderabad (Central University). My doctoral research was carried out at the Center of Excellence in Cyber Security, IDRBT — an institute established by the Reserve Bank of India to strengthen cybersecurity in the financial sector.

I had the privilege of being mentored by Prof. B. M. Mehtre, a pioneer behind India's first Automated Fingerprint Identification System (AFIS), whose work continues to support law enforcement agencies across India and overseas. Working alongside such impactful researcher helped shape my interest in digital forensics and security.

Over the years, my research has led to publications in reputable journals including Elsevier's Forensic Science International (SCI, Q1) and Springer's Signal, Image and Video Processing (SCIE, Q2), as well as multiple Scopus-indexed conference papers and book chapters. My technical contributions also extend to cybersecurity readiness initiatives in the Indian banking sector. At IDRBT, I led and coordinated teams for large-scale cyber drills involving more than 25 participants at a time, helping banks improve their incident response capabilities in line with RBI directives. I also participated in CERT-In national cyber mock drills, worked on forensic lab design, and delivered hands-on security training for banking professionals.

My digital forensics experience includes working with the FRED forensic workstation and industry-standard tools such as EnCase, Oxygen Forensics, and Paraben SIM analysis suites. I hold professional certifications including CISA (Certified Information Systems Auditor) and ISO 27001:2022 ISMS Lead Auditor.

Today, my research spans image forensics and cyber defense analytics. My work continues to be driven by a belief that technology should enable trust — and that education empowers individuals to secure the world they build.

Outside of work, I enjoy exploring new technologies, mentoring enthusiastic learners, and finding creative ways to bring real-world security challenges into the classroom.
